## Current Rules Regarding Caddies

The Rules of Golf (Rule 10) allow a player's own caddie to provide advice throughout a round.

Permitted advice includes:

- Club selection
- Shot strategy
- Wind assessment
- Hazard management
- Green reading
- Course management
- Emotional support
- Distance judgement

Examples include:

- "Take a smooth 7-iron."
- "Aim at the left edge of the bunker."
- "Lay up short of the creek."
- "This putt breaks left."

However, the player remains responsible for:

- Making the stroke
- Executing the shot
- Alignment once taking a stance
- Accepting or rejecting the caddie's advice

This balance preserves golf as a game of individual skill while recognising the value of strategic assistance.

## Equipment Rules

Rule 4.3 governs the use of electronic devices.

Generally permitted:

- GPS distance information
- Laser rangefinders (where Local Rules permit)
- Digital scorecards
- Weather information
- Course maps

Generally prohibited are devices that remove the challenge of the game by:

- Automatically determining the best shot
- Calculating prohibited conditions during play
- Replacing player judgement

The Rules therefore distinguish between **providing information** and **making decisions for the player**.

## The Concept of a Digital Caddie

A Digital Caddie is an AI-powered assistant that performs many of the same advisory functions as a traditional human caddie.

Potential capabilities include:

### Club Recommendation

Analyse:

- Current distance
- Elevation
- Wind
- Temperature
- Player history

Then recommend:

> "Based on your previous shots, a controlled 7-iron is recommended."

---

### Strategy

Advise:

- Lay up versus attack
- Risk versus reward
- Safe landing zones
- Course management

---

### Performance Analysis

Track:

- Carry distances
- Dispersion patterns
- Club consistency
- Miss tendencies
- Pressure performance

Unlike a human caddie, AI can remember every shot ever played.

---

### Learning

A Digital Caddie continually learns:

- Preferred shot shape
- Typical misses
- Club confidence
- Performance by lie
- Performance by weather
- Performance under pressure

This enables increasingly personalised recommendations.

## Human Caddie vs Digital Caddie

| Human Caddie | Digital Caddie |
|--------------|----------------|
| Experience | Statistical learning |
| Course knowledge | Complete course database |
| Emotional support | Objective analysis |
| Memory | Unlimited memory |
| Observation | Sensor and data driven |
| Personal relationship | Personalised AI model |

Rather than replacing human caddies, digital caddies may complement them.

---

## Benefits for Recreational Golf

AI has significant potential to improve golf by helping players:

- Select better clubs
- Make smarter strategic decisions
- Learn course management
- Improve consistency
- Reduce frustration
- Increase pace of play
- Accelerate skill development

Instead of replacing skill, AI can become a coaching tool that helps golfers understand *why* a particular decision is recommended.

---

## Challenges for Competitive Golf

As AI becomes more sophisticated, important questions arise.

Should a Digital Caddie be allowed to:

- Recommend clubs?
- Recommend shot shape?
- Use real-time wind data?
- Analyse strokes gained?
- Learn during the round?
- Simulate multiple shot outcomes?
- Recommend an exact landing position?

These capabilities exceed what even the best human caddie can realistically calculate.

The challenge for golf's governing bodies will be defining where strategic advice ends and artificial optimisation begins.

---

## A Possible Future Framework

One possible model is to classify Digital Caddies into capability levels.

| Level | Capability | Suggested Use |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| 1 | Distance information | Tournament legal |
| 2 | Club recommendation | Recreational play |
| 3 | Strategy using player history | Recreational and coaching |
| 4 | Predictive shot simulation | Coaching only |
| 5 | Fully autonomous decision making | Not permitted during competition |

Such a framework would encourage innovation while preserving the integrity of competitive golf.

---

## Design Principles for a Digital Caddie

An effective Digital Caddie should:

- Assist rather than replace the golfer.
- Explain recommendations rather than simply issuing commands.
- Learn from each player's unique strengths and weaknesses.
- Improve decision making while leaving execution entirely to the player.
- Respect the Rules of Golf and any competition-specific restrictions.

The objective is not to automate golf, but to help golfers make better informed decisions and become better players over time.

---

## Conclusion

The Rules of Golf already recognise that strategic advice is a legitimate and valuable part of the game through the role of the human caddie.

A Digital Caddie extends this concept using modern technology, artificial intelligence, and data analysis. If implemented thoughtfully, it has the potential to become a powerful coaching companion for recreational golfers while preserving the essential challenge of golf: that the player must ultimately choose the shot and execute it.

As AI continues to evolve, the future of Digital Caddies will likely depend not on whether the advisor is human or artificial, but on maintaining the distinction between **assistance** and **automation**.
