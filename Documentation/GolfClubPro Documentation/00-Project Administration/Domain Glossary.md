# Purpose

The Domain Glossary defines the common business language used throughout the GolfClubPro project.

Its purpose is to ensure that developers, testers, designers, product owners and future AI models use consistent terminology.

This glossary is the authoritative source for all domain definitions used within:

- Product Requirements
- Domain Model
- System Architecture
- API Specification
- Database Design
- AI Caddy Design
- Test Plans
- User Documentation

---

# Principles

- Every important business term has one definition.
- Definitions are technology independent.
- Definitions should describe golf concepts, not software implementation.
- Software implementations shall reference glossary terms rather than redefine them.

# Glossary

## AI Caddy

The recommendation engine that analyses historical and current playing conditions to provide personalised advice before each shot.

---

## Aim Point

The intended target location selected before executing a shot.

This may differ from the final target due to wind, hazards or player shot tendencies.

---

## Carry Distance

The horizontal distance travelled by the golf ball before first contacting the ground.

Carry distance excludes roll.

---

## Club

A single item of golfing equipment used to strike the ball.

Examples include:

- Driver
- Fairway Wood
- Hybrid
- Iron
- Wedge
- Putter

Each club may have historical performance metrics unique to an individual player.

---

## Club Recommendation

The club suggested by the AI Caddy based on:

- Distance
- Wind
- Elevation
- Lie
- Historical performance
- Confidence score

---

## Confidence Score

A numerical indication of how confident the AI Caddy is that a recommendation is appropriate.

Future versions may express confidence as a percentage.

---

## Course

A playable golf course consisting of one or more holes, tee sets and local rules.

---

## Course Layout

The complete physical definition of a golf course including:

- Hole geometry
- Tee boxes
- Fairways
- Greens
- Hazards
- GPS reference points

---

## Dispersion

The statistical spread of repeated golf shots.

Dispersion may be measured laterally, longitudinally or radially.

---

## DotGolf Member

A golfer identified using a valid DotGolf membership number.

---

## Equipment Profile

The collection of clubs normally used by a player together with their measured performance characteristics.

---

## Fairway

The closely mown area between the tee and the green intended as the primary landing area.

---

## Green

The prepared putting surface surrounding the hole.

---

## Hazard

Any defined obstacle that influences play.

Examples include:

- Bunkers
- Water
- Trees
- Out of Bounds
- Penalty Areas

---

## Hole

A single playable golf hole.

A hole consists of:

- Tee box
- Fairway
- Hazards
- Green
- Pin location

---

## Hole Detection

Automatic determination of the hole currently being played using GPS, player behaviour and historical movement.

---

## Ideal Landing Area

The preferred landing position for a particular shot considering strategy and player capability.

---

## Lie

The condition and position of the golf ball immediately before a stroke.

Examples include:

- Tee
- Fairway
- Rough
- Sand
- Fringe
- Green

---

## Local Rules

Course-specific rules that supplement the Rules of Golf.

---

## Pin Position

The current location of the hole on the putting green.

---

## Player

A registered golfer using GolfClubPro.

A player may optionally be linked to DotGolf.

---

## Recommendation

Advice generated by the AI Caddy prior to a shot.

A recommendation may include:

- Club
- Aim point
- Target distance
- Wind adjustment
- Coaching note

---

## Round

A complete or partial golf playing session.

A round may consist of:

- 9 holes
- 18 holes
- Practice session
- Training exercise

---

## Shot

A recorded stroke played by a golfer.

Each shot may contain:

- GPS start
- GPS finish
- Club
- Distance
- Direction
- Weather
- Lie
- Timestamp

---

## Shot Pattern

The accumulated historical behaviour of a player's shots with a specific club.

---

## Shot Dispersion

The statistical distribution of shot landing positions.

---

## Stroke

A single intentional movement of a club intended to strike the ball.

---

## Target Line

The intended direction of the shot from the ball to the selected aim point.

---

## Tee Box

The designated starting area for a hole.

---

## Tee Set

A collection of tee boxes defining a course length.

Examples:

- Black
- Blue
- White
- Yellow
- Red

---

## Total Distance

The complete distance travelled by the golf ball including carry and roll.

---

## Weather Context

The environmental conditions affecting a shot.

Includes:

- Wind speed
- Wind direction
- Temperature
- Humidity
- Air pressure

---

## Wind Adjustment

The calculated modification to club selection or aim based on current weather.
